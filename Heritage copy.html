1. Victoria Memorial

Victoria Memorial is located in the heart of the city. It was built in memory of Queen Victoria to celebrate her 25 years of rule over India. It is known to be one of the famous historical places to visit in Kolkata.The beautiful architechural Building is spread over 57 acres of land, housing 21 lush green gardens, 28,394 artefacts and 3,900 artistic paintings.The intricate memorial not only bears testimony to the British rule in India but also stands as an example of advanced and exceptional architecture.

  • Victoria Memorial is Famous For: Exemplary architecture
  • Location wise: Victoria Memorial Hall, 1, Queen’s Way, Kolkata
  • Timings are : 10 am to 5 pm
  • Entry Fee is: INR 20 for Indians, and INR 200 for foreigners

2. Howrah Bridge

Howrah Bridge is a massive steel structure considered as one of the longest cantilever bridges of its type in the world. It is also regarded as one of the busiest bridges among allthat carries a daily traffic of over 100,000 vehicles and countless pedestrians that makes bridge unique is the fact that it was built without a single nut or bolt and is held together by rivets.

  • Bridge is famous for: Being one of the longest cantilever bridges in the world.
  • Location wise: Jagganath Ghat, 1, Strand Road, Kolkata
  • Timings : 24*7
  • Entry Fee :Free entry.

3. Fort William

Fort William is one of the famous historical sites in Kolkatae fnows as tha mighty edifice. It was built in the year 1696. The fort gets its name from King William III and is spread over 70.9 acres. Meticulous stonework adorns the surface of the building, and it took as long as ten years to complete it. Today, Fort William is the property of the Indian Army, and has a capacity of accommodating up to 10,000 army personnel.

  • Fort William is famous for: Its meticulous stonework
  • Location wise: Fort William, Hastings, Kolkata

4. Belur Math

Belur Math is known to be the most famous among many hitorical places. It has been serving as a pilgrimage site, and the headquarters of the Ramakrishna Math and Mission. It was established by Swami Vivekananda and the temple spans over forty acres of land on the west bank of the Hooghly River. The temple is noted for its excellent architecture, whichskillfully and artistically fuses Hindu, Christian, and Islamic motifs together as a symbol of unity of all religions.

  • Balur Math is famous for: Stunning architecture
  • Location wise: Belur, Howrah, West Bengal
  • Timings are : 6 am to 11:30 am, and 4 pm to 7 pm
  • Entry Fee: Free Entry

5. Tagore’s House

People who love the history and Bengali literature, the Jorasanko Thakur Bari or the Tagore’s House is one of the favorite historical places in Kolkata. The home of Nobel Laureate Rabindranath Tagore’s house is now converted into a museum where 700 paintings exhibited in this ancestral home. The museum also houses three different galleries, manuscripts, books, and other antique items.

  • Tagor's House is famous for: Birthplace of Rabindranath Tagore
  • Location wise: Girish Park, Chittaranjan Avenue, Kolkata
  • Timings are : 10 am to 5 pm
  • Entry Fee: INR 10 per person, and INR 5 for students.

6. Indian Museum

Indian Misium is one of the prominent historical places near Kolkata.It is the Indian Museum which is considered as the ninth oldest museum of the world and largest in India. Popularly known as ‘Jadughar’, it unveils some of the finest collection of historical importance ranging from contemporary paintings, a sacred relic of Buddha to the Egyptian mummies. In addition to these, the Indian Museum also boasts of some of the most exquisite collections of ornaments, fossils, skeletons, antiques, armours, and stunning Mughal paintings.Indian Museum is famous for: Exquisite collections of ornaments, antiques and things of historical importance

  • Location: 27, Jawaharlal Nehru Rd, Colootola, New Market Area, Dharmatala, Taltala, Kolkata
  • Timings: 10 am to 5 pm
  • Entry Fee: INR 20 for Indians, and INR 500 for foreigners

7. Birla Planetarium

Birla Planetarium at Kolkata is a circular shaped structure that was designed on the lines of Buddhist Stupa and Sanchi styles.It was established by Pandit Jawaharlal Nehru in 1963. It is one of the most-visited historical places in India.This planetarium hosts a spectacular sky show which is screened every day. Interestingly.Birla Planetarium makes use of equipment which is quite old and is no longer used in the newer facilities.. Birla Planetarium is famous for: Largest planetarium in Asia

  • Location wise: Cathedral Road, near Victoria Memorial, Kolkata
  • Timings are : 11 am to 7 pm
  • Entry Fee: INR 40 per pers

8. Dakshineswar Kali Temple

Tour destination Kolkata is the most stupendous architectural marvels hidden in its lap. Yes! One of such marvels is Dakshineswar Kali Temple that is not only known for its structure but also the old-world charm that it has. Sitting on the eastern banks of River Hooghly, Dakshineswar is dedicated to Goddess Bhavatarani, who happens to be the reincarnation of Goddess Kali. It is often believed that Goddess would help the devotees walk the path of liberation and aid them in achieving redemption. We can observe the Nava-Ratna style adorning this historic masterpiece in Kolkata.

  • Dakshineswar Kali Temple is famous for: Its marvelous architecture
  • Location wise: Dakshineswar, Kolkata, West Bengal
  • Timings are : 6 am to 12:30 pm
  • Entry Fee: Free entry

9. Eden Gardens

Eden Gardens though not a garden is historical place in Kolkata that calls out all the cricket fans in the country. Eden Gardens happens to be the oldest cricket stadium that is still standing proud. In the year 1864, Eden Gardens was considered as the first cricket stadium of the country. It was set by the British during 1841, Eden Gardens is one of the most important historical marvels that is sitting on the floor of Kolkata. It is not only the oldest but also the biggest stadium in the country and can hold around 80,000 people at a time.

  • Eden Garden is famous for: Oldest cricket stadium
  • Location wise: Maidan, B.B.D. Bagh, Kolkata
  • Timings :Na
  • Entry Fee is : Prices start at INR 400

10. Birla Temple

Birla Temple is Owned by G.D. Birla. Birla Temples are spread all over the country and attract pilgrims from various corners. One such Birla temple is located in Kolkata and has now become a historically significant marvel. Birla temple is one of the most revered temples in Kolkata. Temple is dedicated to Lord Lakshmi Narayana, the construction of this temple started in the year 1970 and took approximately 26 years to complete. Built completely in white marble, this eye-catching structure is around 160 ft tall. The perfect blend of spirituality and the architectural beauty can easily be embraced here .

  • Birla Temple is famous for: Remarkable & eye-pleasing architectural design
  • Location wisw : 29, Ashutosh Chowdary Ave, Ballygunge, Kolkata
  • Timings are : 5:30 am to 11 am and 4:30 pm to 9 pm
  • Entry Fee: Free entry
